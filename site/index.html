Understanding the Dashboard

Growth Score:

A normalized score (0-100) representing growth acceleration/uptick compared to previous years. Higher scores indicate neighborhoods experiencing faster growth rates or acceleration, prioritizing emerging areas over established high-volume areas. Based on growth rates, emergence scores, and year-over-year changes rather than absolute counts.

Predicted Licences Issued:

The actual number of new business licences issued predicted for the next year (t+1) based on features from the current year (t). This serves as a proxy for commercial growth.

Scenario Mode (What-if Heuristic):

Run "what-if" analyses by adjusting key indicators. See how hypothetical changes to permits, construction value, or zoning would affect growth predictions and rankings. Note: Scenario mode uses an approximate adjustment heuristic and does not re-run the trained model. It is best used for directional exploration rather than exact forecast changes.

Methodology

What is being forecast: This dashboard forecasts new business licences issued, which serve as a proxy for neighbourhood-level commercial growth. The model predicts the number of new business licences issued in year (t+1) based on features from year t.

Data sources: Business activity signals (licences issued, active businesses), development indicators (permits, construction value), zoning capacity (area-weighted zoning shares), and vibrancy metrics (pedestrian/bicycle counts, if available). All data comes from City of Edmonton Open Data.

Model approach: Gradient Boosting (LightGBM) with time-based train/test split. Trained on earlier years, tested on most recent 1-2 years to ensure no future data leakage.

Interpretation: Results should be used for exploratory planning and screening. Predictions are probabilistic, not guarantees. Explanations indicate correlation, not causation.

Limitations: Forecasts new business licences issued, not revenue or business success. Uses open-data proxies and historical patterns. Zoning treated as static. Scenario mode is approximate. Intended for exploratory planning and screening, not causal claims or guaranteed outcomes.
